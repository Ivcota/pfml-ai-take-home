Paid Family and Medical Leave System Product Brief

The state is rolling out a new Paid Family and Medical Leave program and needs a robust software system to serve as the backbone of the program. Employers have been making payroll contributions for more than a year, employees will be able to apply in the coming months, and state staff are prepared to adjudicate those claims as they come in. The state expects their Paid Family and Medical Leave program to operate similar to the existing programs in other states across the US, but have highlighted the following requirements in particular.

PROGRAM REQUIREMENTS

Employees can apply for up to 20 weeks of leave in a single year and when approved will be paid the same weekly wage they had previously been making. Employees can apply for two different kinds of leave: 1) bonding with a child, and 2) medical leave.

Leave can be denied in the following situations:
The employee has already taken 20 weeks of leave
The employee has not earned any wages in the past year
At the employer’s discretion

When employees go to apply, they must supply: SSN, FEIN of employer, reason for leave, leave dates, payment preferences (check/direct deposit, address, bank account information, etc.) and documentation (birth certificate, doctor’s note, etc.).

Employees will get paid on a weekly basis. The amount they are paid is based on the last 4 quarters of wages with a ceiling on the amount they can receive that is set by the state.

ADDITIONAL CONSIDERATIONS

The system should manage employee leave applications, eligibility verification, and weekly payment processing. The design should include core components needed to securely handle sensitive personal information, calculate payments based on wage history, and distribute funds through multiple payment methods.

GRC (governance, risk, and compliance) will need to be addressed before go-live, but does not need to be detailed at the moment. However, please identify aspects of the system that may be subject to additional GRC scrutiny. Additionally, be prepared to discuss architecture choices, data models, and how the system would scale to handle thousands of concurrent applications. Assume AWS and feel free to use any other SAAS product you'd like.
